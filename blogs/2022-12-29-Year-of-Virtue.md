# Year of Virtue

In the spirit of CGPGrey's [New Year's Themes](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NVGuFdX5guE) I'm going to make 2023 the year of *virtue*. 

Off the back of thinking and finding some utilitarian bullets that I'm reluctant to bite but a desire to have moral conisitency - I have some intuitions that virtue ethics may prove fruitful. My main issue in trying to have a consist moral framework comes from the axioms that you base that framework on - 'suffering bad, pleasure good' is a pretty solid foundation that I struggle to see issue with and is why utilitariansim has appealed. I believe I need to endeavour to find a list of virtues that are still grounded in the axiom of suffering being a moral bad (for anyone/thing that experiences the suffering) and that pleasure is a moral good.

Looking at [virtue ethics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtue_ethics) some of the problems I feel I need to work out inculde:

- The 'Golden Mean' seemingly being a single value to find - intuitively it feels like this may be better thought of as a 'Golden Range' to account for uncertainty and balancing the different virtues.
- Deciding on a consistent/justified list of virtues grounded in well-reasoned axioms (likely 'suffering bad, pleasure good').
- One idea of having 'utility' be a virtue - what this would actually mean (as a virtue)?

I think my aim will be to finalise a list of virtues and answer these questions within the next year - with the aim to use this to make consistent morally good actions going forward.
