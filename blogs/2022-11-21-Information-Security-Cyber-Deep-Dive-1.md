# Information Security - Cyber Deep Dive #1

## What is Information Security?

Information security (or InfoSec) forms part of wider cyber security. It [refers exculisivly to the processes designed for data security](https://www.cisco.com/c/en/us/products/security/what-is-information-security-infosec.html). It is the [practice of protecting information and mitigating risk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_security), information security focuses on the CIA triad (confidentiality, integrity and availability).

## How is Information Security Achieved?

Information security can be achieved through the use of an [ISMS (Information Security Management System)](https://www.cisco.com/c/en/us/products/security/what-is-information-security-infosec.html) - the guidelines, procedures and processes that an organisation implements to mitigate and respond to data breaches; for instance following [ISO 27001](https://www.iso.org/isoiec-27001-information-security.html).

Theses [processes can include](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_security):

- Assest identification
- Threat and vulnerability identification
- Risk evaluation
- Risk management - leading to risk mitigation, risk avoidance or acceptence of risks
- Designing and implementing security controls for risk mitigation
- Continuous monitoring and improvement of current information management procedures

## Information Security Core Principles

The core principles of information security are Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability. The balance of these principles is integral to an organisation's cyber security but they cann appear to be at odds with each other - for instance, encrypting data can ensure good confidentiality but if the data cannot be decrypted this would be bad for availability.

### Confidentiality

Confidentiality is the principle of [preventing unauthorised access to information](https://www.imperva.com/learn/data-security/information-security-infosec/#:~:text=What%20are%20the%203%20Principles,are%20called%20the%20CIA%20Triad.). For example - protecting proprietary data, personal information or user passwords.

### Integrity

Integrity is the principle of ensuring information is not edited or lost when not authorised - maintaining the [accuracy and completeness of data over its entire lifecycle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_security#Integrity). For instance - implementing access control and using file hashes.

### Availability

Availability is the principle of ensuring systems and information remain accessible to authorised users. For instance ensuring websites remain online by protecting against [DOS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denial-of-service_attack) attacks. 

## Non-Repudiation

Non-repudiation is the idea that [someone cannot deny the validity of something](https://www.cryptomathic.com/products/authentication-signing/digital-signatures-faqs/what-is-non-repudiation). It is primarily a legal concept but is used in information security, for example, through the use of digital signatures - a technique to verify the authenticity and integrity of messages or documents.
