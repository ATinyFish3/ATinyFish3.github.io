# Why is There Not More Terrorism?

Recently there was an attack involving petrol bombs being at a Dover migrant centre [[1]](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-kent-63473640) which got me thinking; why does this sort of thing not happen more often?

The attacker used "petrol bombs with fireworks attached" - things which anyone can get there hands on incredibly easy. Furthermore, the attacker seems to have been motivated by racism - something which is, unfortunately, not in small supply. Additionally it seems that, especially in more recent years, when a terror attack does happen that it is extremely ineffective.

## Good Reasons that there may be Less Terrorism than "Expected"

### Intelligence Services

One explanation is that the Intelligence Services stop many attacks before they have the opportunity to take place - last year MI5 said that they had foiled 31 late-stage terror plots in the last four years [[2]](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-58512901). This, to me, still seems like a low number of potential terror attacks in the first place. However, another factor that results from this is that potential terrorists are put off from carrying out acts of terror due to the risk of being caught, or the risk of communicating with others about plotting an act of terror is too high - and lone actors have fewer resources or are less likely to actually go ahead with any ideas.

### Lack of Violent Predisposition

If all 3% of the US population who think that killing civilians (in the context of extremist actions) is often justified [[3]](https://www.pewresearch.org/religion/2017/07/26/findings-from-pew-research-centers-2017-survey-of-us-muslims/) went on to actually commit an act of terror then there would be (332M*.03) 9,960,000 American terrorists. In actuality, in the last decade there have been 15 terrorist attacks in the US - about 0.0002% of the possible 9,960,000. The very overwhelming majority of people who think acts of terror are often justified never act on this belief.

## Why are Terror Attacks so Ineffective

### 2016 Nice Truck Attack

In 2016 a 19-tonne cargo truck was driven into crowds of people in Nice, France killing 86 and injuring 458. This is a particularly terrifying example of an already scary terror act - the reason I believe this to be so scary is that we see trucks everyday, or vans or cars - the barrier to entry for [vehicle-ramming attacks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vehicle-ramming_attack) is incredibly, scarily low. However, this is also somewhat of an outlier; the average number of people killed inn these types of attack is 5 [[4]](https://www.rand.org/blog/2017/06/why-arent-there-more-terrorist-attacks-like-the-one.html)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vehicle-ramming_attack#List_of_attacks). Maybe the Nice attack was worse because the attack used a truck - however the [2017 Stockholm truck attack](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Stockholm_truck_attack) killed 5 people. How is it that most attacks kill ~17 times less people than in Nice?
In 2015 [hackers were able to remotely carjack a Jeep](https://www.independent.co.uk/news/science/hackers-remotely-carjack-jeep-from-10-miles-away-and-drive-it-into-ditch-10406554.html) but I cannot find any attacks using this technique. Why have we not seen any attacks like this in the last 7 years?

I do not have an answer to these questions but a shot in the dark intuition: perhaps most people that have the intelligence and skills necessary to commit effective acts of terror are also intelligent enough not to do so.

Maybe we're just lucky.
