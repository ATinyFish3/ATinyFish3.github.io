# Guilty Giving; Good?

## WHy Feel Guilty in the First Place?

I think the obvious, intuitive answer to this question is that people feel guilty that they are comparatively better off than those in poorer places or less fortunate circumstances. I, personally, do not think this *is* a good reason to feel guilty; noone has done something that has lead to this difference in circumstances - it's just dumb (un)luck. Furthermore, what is the guilt actually for? If you feel guilt that you are better off - don't feel happy that you get to live a happy life, unprecadented in human history (for the most part). If you feel guilt for people in worse circumstances - why? You had no hand in this - of course there are plenty of justified negative emotions that can be felttowards this disparity, I just don't think that guilt is one of them.

## Why Not Feeling Guilt is a Good Thing

Not feeling guilty about those less fortunate than you can be beneficial - if you want to do something about them being less fortunate. Imagine that your motivation for  wanting to help people is guilt - surely the act of donating money to charity, as a way to help, would be in itself enough to relieve this guilt. Now imageine your motivation is because you think suffering is bad or that your morals compel you to do so - you may be more motivated to do the most good you can
